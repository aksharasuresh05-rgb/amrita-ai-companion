Explain Ohm's Law with an example.

Ohm's Law states that the current flowing through a conductor is directly proportional to the voltage across it, provided the temperature remains constant. Formula: V = I × R Where: V → Voltage I → Current R → Resistance Example: If R = 10Ω and V = 20V, Current = 20 / 10 = 2A
